What is a Volcanic Eruption?

A volcanic eruption occurs when volcanoes spew hot, dangerous gasses, ash, lava, and rock that are powerfully destructive on the Earth’s surface. Health risks from volcanic eruptions include floods, mudslides, power outages, contaminated drinking water, and wildfires.

Preparedness Measures:

Knowing how and why volcanic hazards occur and the places most likely to be affected is the first step in protecting people, property, and investments from these risks.

  • Comply strictly with PHIVOLCS‘ prohibition against human settlement in permanent danger zones. Always be on alert and listen to advisories of not venturing into identified restricted areas during volcanic unrest.
  • Identify, evaluate, and characterize areas vulnerable to hazards and implement appropriate land use plan and zonation.
  • Heed warnings and evacuation orders in times of volcanic unrest.

What to do before, during, and after a Volcanic Eruption

Before:

  1. Listen to news updates for information about an impending eruption that could result in ashfall.
  2. Prepare an emergency kit and place it in an accessible area.
  3. Prepare a gas mask or clean cloth to cover the nose and goggles to protect the eyes.
  4. Prepare a gas mask or clean cloth to cover the nose and goggles to protect the eyes.

During:

  1. Stay calm and stay indoors until it is safe to go outside.
  2. Use a damp, clean cloth or gas mask to cover the nose and mouth.
  3. Listen to the news for the latest updates regarding the volcanic eruption.
  4. Keep pets inside the house to prevent them from inhaling ash.
  5. Close all windows and doors of the house.
  6. If driving a vehicle, pull to the side of the road and stop if there is heavy ashfall and eep the windows up and do not use the air conditioning system.
  7. If outdoors, look for shelter and put on eye protection such as goggles. Avoid wearing contact lenses.

After

  1. Listen to news updates to know when it is safe to return after an eruption and when it is safe to go outside.
  2. If an individual has breathing problems such as asthma or other lung conditions, avoid contact with ash and take precautions in areas with poor air quality, as exposure may pose serious health risks.
  3. Wear protective clothing and a mask when cleaning up.
  4. Scrape the ash from the roof of the house as soon as the ashfall stops to avoid damage or collapse.
  5. Remove and clean ash from windows, doors, and other parts of the house.
  6. Shake loose ash from plants before watering them.
  7. Use powder detergent when washing clothing that has been exposed to ash.
  8. Collect the ashes and place them far from any water drainage to prevent clogging.
