What is a Fire?

A fire is when something burns destructively and emits heat, light, flame, and smoke. Fires frequently occur as a result of negligence, irresponsible behavior, or defects in technology or products. Fire generates a black, impenetrable smoke that blocks vision and stings the eyes. It is impossible to navigate through such smoke, so fire drill participants should practice evacuating houses/buildings by at least two routes.

What to do before, during, and after a Fire

Before:

  1. Establish an emergency fire plan.
  2. Install systems to alert people about fire such as smoke alarms and detectors, and sprinkler systems.
  3. Have defined evacuation routes in case of emergency.
  4. Have regular evacuation drills to know what to do during a fire disaster.
  5. Train individuals on how to use fire extinguishers.
  6. Learn fire safety precautions and ensure that everyone is aware of the STOP, DROP, and ROLL method.

During:

  1. Follow the emergency fire plan to get and stay out of fire.
  2. Make an immediate call to the fire department.
  3. Crawl under low smoke.
  4. Be aware of the evacuation routes.

After:

  1. Return home only when the authorities have determined that your place is safe.
  2. Continuously check news reports for further instructions and emergency updates.
  3. Check for safety and use extreme caution when entering a burned area.
  4. Take precautions when cleaning your property and wear protective gears.
