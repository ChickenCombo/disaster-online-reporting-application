What is a Tropical Cyclone?

An intense low pressure system with minimum sustained winds of 35 kilometers per hour is referred to as a tropical cyclone or "bagyo" (kph). Strong winds and heavy rainfall from tropical cyclones can result in widespread flooding and flash floods, as well as storm surges, landslides, and mudflows.

Public Storm Warning Signal #1

Precautionary measures:

  • This signal might be upgraded to the following level when the tropical cyclone is strong or is intensifying and approaching.
  • The size and height of the waves in coastal waters may gradually increase.
  • Every six hours, PAGASA issues a severe weather bulletin, and the public is advised to listen to it. Business can continue as usual in the meantime, with the exception of times when there are floods.

Public Storm Warning Signal #2

Precautionary measures:

  • Small seacraft are at risk in the ocean and coastal waters.
  • The storm may intensify and move closer to the area, so it is important to pay close attention to its most recent position, direction, and speed of movement.
  • The general public is advised to avoid unnecessary risks, especially those who travel by air and sea.
  • Children's outdoor activities should be postponed.
  • Before the signal is upgraded, secure the properties.
  • Disaster preparedness organizations and agencies are working to inform their local communities.

Public Storm Warning Signal #3

Precautionary measures:

  • The communities that are threatened or affected by the disturbance are in danger.
  • All seacraft will be in very dangerous conditions in the sea and coastal waters.
  • Travel is extremely dangerous, especially by air and sea.
  • It is advised that people stay away from the coast and river banks, evacuate low-lying areas, and seek refuge in sturdy buildings.
  • Be on the lookout for the typhoon's "eye," which can be identified by an abrupt change from very bad weather to fair weather, with very strong winds typically coming from the north.
  • Do not leave a safe shelter when the typhoon's "eye" strikes the area; the worst weather will return in one to two hours with extremely strong winds coming from the south.
  • Classes in all levels should be suspended, and kids should stay inside secure structures for their protection.
  • Organizations and agencies that deal with preparedness for and response to disasters are operating and responding to emergencies as they arise.

Public Storm Warning Signal #4

Precautionary measures:

  • The situation has the potential to be very dangerous to the neighborhood.
  • Travel and outdoor activities should both be canceled.
  • Since it might already be too late in this situation, evacuation to safer shelters ought to have been completed.
  • The concerned disaster management councils and other disaster response organizations are now fully prepared to respond immediately to a potential calamity and are responding to emergencies.

What to do in case of typhoons

  • Remain calm and stay indoors.
  • Watch for news on TV and the radio.
  • Secure your home.
  • Trim the trees close to homes
  • Roads should be clear for emergency vehicles.
  • If your residence is located in a flood-prone area, proceed to the closest designated evacuation center.
  • Have a radio and a flashlight on hand, both with new batteries.
  • Stock up on supplies for first aid, batteries, gas, food, and potable water.
  • Turn off your home's primary sources of gas, electricity, and water in case of flooding.
  • Place furniture higher than the anticipated flood level. Keep out of the way of floodwaters all appliances, valuables, chemicals, toxic materials, and garbage.
  • Avoid foothills, cliffs, riverbanks, creeks, coastal areas, and low-lying areas. Rain can trigger landslides, rockslides, and mudslides.
  • Do not wade through areas that are flooded. Do not try to cross moving water.
  • During a flood, never use any electrical equipment.
  • Electrical or gas appliances that have been flooded should not be used.
